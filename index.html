The Context

isoshealth’s aim was to present a digital interface to it’s clients that would enable them to choose a dedicated team and carry out online consultations according to their preferred time. It also wanted to enable the practitioners to collaborate effectively in a multidisciplinary team and help clients to acheive their health goals.

The Approach

The expectation was to get the product out in the market within 3-4 months, so we decided to go for lean approach that includes rapid sketching, prototyping, design mockups and user feedback.

The Process

Discovery Phase
    We began by understanding the following:
  • What the clients wanted to achieve with the product
  • What the users want to achieve with the product
  • Who the users are and how they’d use the product; their age, occupation, personality, characteristics, behaviour patterns, etc
  • Features that the clients want to implement and how they are relevant to the goals. Any competitors and how isoshealth is different from others.
  • What are the different constraints related to technology, deadline, etc.
After discussions with the isoshealth team, we sat down with our technical team to come up with a bird’s eye view of our application
Crafting the experience

After gathering insights from the previous phase, we assessed how best the features/functionality that the client aim to implement would add to the user experience of the product and help achieve their goals and their users’ goals. We came up with various users personas and then crafted user journeys for each of our personas to conceptualise the proposed functionality.

Overall User journeys for enrolment manager, practitioner/expert and client
Sketch it up

We explored various ideas for layout, navigation and interaction by sketching and creating paper mockups.

We went through all ideas and weighed them against our requirements, platform-specific design guidelines, established design patterns, basic design principles and our constraints to decide on the best.

Sketches for practitioner's dashboard
Design and interaction

We went through the brand guidelines and designed the visuals of few key screens using appropriate colours, typography, and icons in Sketch app.

After presenting those screens to the clients, we agreed upon a visual style and designed the rest of the screens

Further, we created interactive prototypes using invision. We encouraged the participation of the clients to give feedback for the designs on invision.

Interactive prototypes expert/dashboard on invision
Coding templates

After incorporating feedback from the clients, I created the HTML and SASS templates in reactstrap which were handed over to the development team to carry out development in react, redux and node.js

The Refinement - User testing

We also carried out user testing with some practitioners on our development environment. We got some good insights which helped us to further refine the overall experience of the application
