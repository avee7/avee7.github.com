Our aim was to deliver a easy-to-use web interface that would allow clients and health care practitioners to have effective video consultations. The interface would reflect the power of digital platforms as a way to collaborate and break geographical boundaries.

The Context

isoshealth’s aim was to present a digital interface to it’s clients that would enable them to choose a dedicated team and carry out online consultations according to their preferred time. It also wanted to enable the practitioners to collaborate effectively in a multidisciplinary team and help clients to acheive their health goals.

The Approach

The expectation was to get the product out in the market within 3-4 months, so we decided to go for lean approach that includes rapid sketching, prototyping, design mockups and user feedback.

The Process

Discovery Phase

We began the product discovery by understanding core aspects of the application, like, what the clients want to achieve, what the users want to achieve, type of users and how they would use the application, etc.

Next, our sessions involved whiteboarding and sketching of key features which the clients wanted to implement.

Crafting the experience

After gathering insights from the previous phase, we assessed how best the features that the client aim to implement would add to the user experience of the product and help achieve their goals and their users’ goals. We came up with various users personas and then crafted user journeys for each of our personas to conceptualise the proposed functionality.

A glimpse of client user journey to join platform
Sketch it up

We explored various ideas for layout, navigation and interaction by sketching and creating paper mockups.

We went through these ideas and weighed them against our requirements, design patterns, basic design principles and our constraints to decide on the best.

Sketches for practitioner's dashboard
Design and interaction

We went through the brand guidelines and designed the visuals of few key screens using appropriate colours, typography, and icons in Sketch.

After presenting those screens to the clients, we got quick feedback on the visual style and then designed the rest of the screens.

Next, I created interactive prototypes in invision. Digital Prototypes helped me to get feedback and approval from clients and the senior leadership.

Coding templates

After incorporating feedback from the clients and seniors, I created the HTML and SASS templates in reactstrap which were handed over to the development team.

The Refinement - User testing

We also carried out user testing with some practitioners on our development environment. We got some good insights which helped us to further refine the overall experience of the application.

Product Design

The Practitioner Dashboard

The practitioner's dashboard comprised of upcoming tasks view, an active to-do task, a stream of past posts and a sidebar containing profile information and links to clients, availability, teams and sessions booked.

Client's Journal View

The journal view helped the practitioner to see list of all the sessions arranged for the client in the package, client's questionnaire, ongoing package and the enrolled team. The practitioner can view his/her scheduled session in the active to-do task box.

Session Rebook Request

The client can request to rebook a session. The 'Rebook' link on each scheduled session on the task list enables him/her to rebook a session. The modal consists of some predefined reasons, a free text field to add additional comments and a booking matrix to view date and time according to the availability of the practitioner.

Team Selection in Package Purchase

After entering the time preference, the client can select a multidisciplinary team based on his/her choice.
