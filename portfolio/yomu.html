Work in progress

A website where a user can find all the content from over 100 sources based upon their interests, topics, authors or publishers.

This side project is being designed with the aim to sharpen my end-to-end skills in the design process.

Background

This project is being developed by an ex-colleague, Jasdeep Madan, from Thoughtworks. He wanted to build a one-stop destination for people to read personalised content for which he had already built a website.

My role in the project is defining user experience of the product and designing its interface. Since the project is work in progress, I’m going to share one design solution and the journey to build it.

Early concept testing helped me to understand which concepts better define our product according to the business, which eventually helped me to move in a particular direction.

User Research

The goal of our user research was to understand the behaviour of the user as well validation of the existing features.

So, I created a method that comprised of surveys, 15 minutes user interview and 45 minutes usability testing.

Creating User Persona

Based on the research, we identified that the customer segment comprised of IT professionals aged 25-44 years.

Key insights for a typical user were:

  • They want to save time
  • They wish to see relevant info
  • They aim to see articles from a reliable source
  • They look for recommended articles
Competitive Analysis

Based on the users’ votes, Google News, HackerNews, Twitter, Medium were the most popular to find content.

I also analysed some similar products - PaperOak, Feedly, Google’s Discover which helped me to understand what they offer and what is the rational behind them.

Design and Interaction

We went through the brand guidelines and designed the visuals of few key screens using appropriate colours, typography, and icons in Sketch.

After presenting those screens to the clients, we got quick feedback on the visual style and then designed the rest of the screens.

Next, we created interactive prototypes in Invision which helped us to get valuable feedback by user testing. The prototypes also helped to get feedback and approval from clients and the senior leadership.

Designing the product: Sketch, Creating interactive prototypes: Invision
Coding templates

After incorporating feedback from the users, clients and seniors, templates in HTML and SASS were created to deliver to the development team.

Continuous communication and collaboration between the designers and developers helped us to discuss ideas and concerns which, consequently, removed many blockers while development.

User Testing

We invited some practitioners and carried out user testing on our development environment. We got some good insights which helped us to further refine the overall experience of the application.
