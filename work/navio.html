Responsive Framework

Navio was built on a fixed framework. The challenge was to transform it into a responsive app which could work on various screens. The student's app was targeted for mobile phones and the teacher's app was targeted for the big displays (projectors, TVs, whiteboards, etc.) which could enhance the classroom experience.

I worked on building up a responsive ecosystem for the product. This ecosystem comprises of:

  • Responsive Grids
  • Responsive Typography
  • Uniform and fluid spacing

With the help of interactive prototypes, I was able to validate my decisions while building up the framework. Here's a preview of the guidelines for the developers and the designers.

Building an accessible and inclusive design

Designing an app which would be usable and accessible to all has been an important concern for the team since a long time. I got an opportunity to lead the accessiblity efforts in our design team. I created guidelines which helped the design and content team to install an inclusive design mentality.

UX accessibility guidelines preview

Leading accessiblity in the team helped me to be more aware while designing my products. I further shared the guidelines in the form an article in UX collective which would help designers to build more accessible products.

The article 'Accessibility guidelines for UX designers' got a good response from the readers. I was very grateful to receive such a positive response from everywhere round the globe.
